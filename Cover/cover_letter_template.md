# Cover Letter Template

Use this as the adaptive layer for role-specific covers. Keep the tone grounded, direct, and human.

## Writing Rules

- Use the same facts as the CV.
- Lead with purpose and fit, not buzzwords.
- Use neutral verbs: led, designed, implemented, built, established, partnered.
- Anchor claims in systems, environments, and outcomes.
- Keep the letter readable aloud. If a sentence sounds inflated, cut it.

## Four-Paragraph Structure

### 1. Opening

State the role, why it matters to you, and the strongest fit in one paragraph.

Example frame:

I am writing to apply for the [Role Title] role at [Organisation]. With more than 15 years of experience across enterprise data, analytics, governance, and platform delivery in complex public and institutional environments, I have built trusted decision-support systems that help senior leaders turn strategy into measurable outcomes.

### 2. Current Role Alignment

Use the strongest current-role evidence that matches the JD.

Example prompts:

- current leadership scope
- executive partnering
- platform and governance responsibility
- planning and reporting outcomes

### 3. Cross-Sector Proof

Use one short paragraph to show this is not a one-role story.

Example prompts:

- public health analytics and forecasting
- multi-agency data platform delivery
- government reporting and compliance
- regulated audit environments

### 4. Motivation and Close

Connect your style of leadership to the organisation's mission and values, then ask for the conversation.

## Quick Fill Template

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am writing to apply for the [Role Title] role at [Organisation]. With more than 15 years of experience across enterprise data architecture, analytics, governance, and platform enablement, I have built trusted data and decision-support capability that helps senior leaders translate strategy into measurable outcomes. I would welcome the opportunity to bring that experience to [Organisation].

In my current role as [Current Role], I [strongest current alignment statement]. I have led work across [platforms / governance / planning / reporting], while partnering with [stakeholders] to establish [trusted metrics / governance / operating model / executive insight].

Earlier in my career, I [cross-sector evidence statement]. These roles strengthened my ability to [relevant capability], while keeping my work grounded in delivery, trust, and practical outcomes rather than theory.

I am particularly drawn to [Organisation / mission / role purpose]. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background in [top three matching capabilities] could support [organisation outcome].

Yours sincerely,

Andrei Stoian

## Final Check

- Does each paragraph map to the JD?
- Could you explain every sentence with a real example?
- Did you keep the tone factual rather than promotional?
