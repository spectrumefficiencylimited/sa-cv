## Extra Notes From Stefan's Second Video

This second video is less about profile writing and more about how LinkedIn activity should support the profile.

### What matters for your profile

- Stay bottom-funnel, not broad. The profile should attract recruiters and decision-makers for specific leadership roles, not try to appeal to everyone.
- Show authority through concrete work: platforms built, governance implemented, planning enabled, teams led.
- Keep cognitive load low. Short paragraphs, clear wording, and no unnecessary jargon stacking.
- Keep the tone human. You do not need to sound over-polished or AI-generated.

### What you should post about on LinkedIn

- Screenshots or short stories about governance, reporting, or architecture work you have done.
- Lessons from building Azure Synapse, Purview, Power BI, forecasting, or planning capability.
- Short reflections on real problems solved: data trust, executive reporting, privacy, stewardship, AI readiness, delivery standards.
- Before/after thinking: what changed because of a platform, governance decision, or reporting improvement.
- Stories from leadership work: mentoring analysts, setting delivery standards, aligning business and IT, translating strategy into data products.

### Good post direction for you

- `How we made executive reporting more trusted by fixing governance, not just dashboards`
- `What Microsoft Purview is actually useful for in a real enterprise environment`
- `Why planning and forecasting problems are usually data operating model problems first`
- `Three things that make a data platform usable by leaders, not just engineers`
- `What changed when we treated data governance as an enabler instead of a compliance exercise`
- `A delivery standard I introduced that improved BI quality without slowing the team down`
